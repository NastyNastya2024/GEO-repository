FAQ on brand promotion in AI and search

Top-level marketing and business requests - in ten intents. The answers are boiled down to the essence; details - follow the links to sections of the site.

Key observation: most questions fit into a chain Visibility → Influence → Control → Measurement - first “we are not here”, then “why”, “how to get there”, “how to manage”, “how to calculate the result”.

1. Strategic intent - how to build a system

Hidden request: “Give me a model of how it works now.”

  • How to promote a brand in 2026? — Combine classic search (SEO), answers and snippets (AEO), citations in LLM (GEO) and an agreed “canon” of facts on the site (AIO); social networks are an amplifier, not the only source.
  • How to build a brand promotion strategy? — Record audience intentions, entity map (product, geography, evidence), method priorities and content + technology roadmap.
  • How to promote through neural networks / AI / LLM? — Provide extractable facts, answer pages, tables and FAQs; be cited on your domain and in external authoritative pages.
  • What is the difference between SEO and AEO/GEO? — SEO — about search ranking; AEO - about the “question → short answer” format and snippets; GEO is about getting a brand into generative responses and the training circuit of models.
  • How should a brand look in ChatGPT / Gemini / search? — Uniform wording, clear differences from competitors, honest product limitations, current dates and links to the source on your website.

Further: Strategic intent, implementation of GEO/AEO, review of methods, ChatGPT, Gemini.

2. Visibility intent - where are we anyway?

Hidden request: “Do we exist in the digital field or not?”

  • How can I check if ChatGPT is responding to my brand? — Make a list of prompts (category, comparison, “best in town”), record responses and cited URLs several times a week.
  • Why is the brand not shown in AI responses? — Often there are no answer pages, weak external correlation with the topic, contradictory facts, or the domain is not included in the retrieval sample.
  • Where is the brand mentioned on the Internet? — Maps, marketplaces, media, reviews, partners, social networks, catalogues; Consistency of name, address and services is important.
  • How to analyze visibility in neural networks? — Prompts + screenshots/logs + list of cited domains; compare with competitors and your URLs.
  • How to check a company's digital presence? — Go through the checklist: website, local profiles, structured data, external mentions, social networks, NAP/offer matches.

Further: AI visibility audit, analytics and audit, industries and neurosearch.

3. Intent of influence - how to get into the answers

Hidden request: “How to become a source of truth for a model.”

  • How to get into ChatGPT/AI replies? — Become useful for retrieval: clear question-answer blocks, comparisons, primary data, internal linking of the topic.
  • How to make a neural network recommend a product? — Explicit selection criteria, cases, restrictions, prices/conditions without slogans; duplicate trust on reputable sites.
  • How to manage reputation in AI? — Eliminate discrepancies between the site and external sources; respond to negativity with facts; enhance expert materials.
  • How are LLM responses formed and how to influence them? — Retrieval + generalization; influence is through content, structure, citations and freshness of signals, not through “keywords” alone.
  • What influences brand mentions? — Relevance of the page to the request, authority of the domain/page, repetition of the fact in independent sources, absence of contradictions.

Further: GEO, AEO, audit and metrics, Perplexity and sources.

4. Content intent - what to create

Hidden request: “What to produce so that there is a result.”

  • What content is needed to promote your brand? — Guides “how to choose”, comparisons, FAQ, cases with numbers, pages with one clear intent of the request.
  • What articles should I write to get cited? — With tables, steps, restrictions and links to primary data; title = wording of the user's question.
  • How to write for intentions? - One page - one main question; Above is a short answer, below are details and objective alternatives.
  • What is a “content factory”? — Regulations: topics from search and AI prompts → page templates → proofreading of facts → publication and measurement.
  • What formats work: guides, comparisons, FAQ? - Yes; plus checklists, “myth vs fact”, calculators and glossary pages of entities.

Further: content factory, page system, AEO and formats.

5. Technical intent - optimization

Hidden request: “How to make machines read us.”

  • How to optimize a website for AI / LLM? — Clean URL structure, HTML hierarchy of headings, accessible text (not only in PDF/images), speed and mobile layout.
  • Do you need structured data? - Yes, where appropriate: Organization, Product, FAQPage, LocalBusiness - as a hint to parsers and searches; does not replace meaningful text.
  • How to improve indexing? — Sitemap, internal links, canonicals, absence of duplicates, clear statuses in Search Console.
  • How to speed up a website for SEO/AEO? — LCP, less blocking JS, cache, image compression, stable hosting.
  • How to link the site and external sources? — Identical legal and product formulations; from the site - links to official profiles and back where possible.

Further: AIO, SEO, code checklist in analytics, technical implementation block.

6. Intent of comparison - we vs competitors

Hidden request: “Why not us and what they do better.”

  • Why are competitors in AI and we are not? — They often have denser thematic clusters, more independent citations, or stronger response pages to your own queries.
  • How to analyze competitors in AI search? - The same prompts as for yourself; table: who is cited, what URLs, what content formats.
  • Who dominates the niche? — Look by AI results and classic search; often the leaders coincide, but not always.
  • Which brands are most recommended? — With well-resolved entities, fresh cases and less noise in the facts.

Further: audit layers, platform comparison, AI‑discovery.

7. Intent of trust and reputation

Hidden request: "How to become a credible entity."

  • How to increase trust in a brand? — Transparent contacts, real cases, expert signatures, consistency of the offer everywhere.
  • How is reputation formed in AI? — From a corpus of texts about you; contradictions reduce the likelihood of an affirmative recommendation.
  • What sources are considered authoritative? — Official website, regulators and standards, media, scientific/industry databases, large marketplaces — depending on the topic.
  • How to work with reviews and mentions? — Respond to the point, correct facts on the site and on the website; Encourage detailed feedback.

Further: implementation and channels, AI+SMM, company and contacts.

8. Channel content - where to post

Hidden request: “Where to be so that they can see us.”

  • Where to publish content for AI? — On a domain that is indexed; plus channels where your niche is actually quoted from (industry media, specialized Q&A, partners).
  • Which platforms are indexed better? — Depends on the niche; check which URLs actually fall into the responses of your prompts.
  • Reddit / Medium / LinkedIn - do they work? - As secondary signals and for narrow topics - yes; as a replacement for your own website - no.
  • Do you need PR publications? — Yes, if they provide high-quality mentions and do not duplicate spam; What matters is the fact and context, not the number of tape notes.

Further: channels on the roadmap, social networks and articles, platforms.

9. Intent metrics and ROI

Hidden request: “How to prove to business that this is a system, not magic.”

  • How to measure the effectiveness of AI promotion? — Share of prompts with mentions/recommendations, cited URLs, traffic from AI search, leads by tags.
  • What metrics are important? — AI Visibility Rate, intent coverage, accuracy of facts, dynamics of competitive comparisons.
  • How to calculate ROI? — Link the cost of content and work to the increase in quality visits and transactions from tagged channels.
  • How do you know if a strategy is working? — Increase in positive responses to target prompts + improvement in classic SEO indicators where they are related.

Further: GEO/AEO metrics, BI dashboard, tracking and cycles.

10. Implementation intent - how to implement

Hidden request: “How to turn it into an action plan.”

  • Where to start promoting your brand? — Audit of facts and visibility → list of intentions → quick answer pages for top prompts.
  • What are the first steps? — Align NAP and offer, close technical blockers, release 3-5 supporting articles on the main issues.
  • How to build a roadmap? — Quarters: foundation → content clusters → external citations → measurement and iteration.
  • How much does it cost? — Depends on the volume of pages and niche; cheaper iterations with measurements than one-time “big text” without metrics.
  • What specialists are needed? — SEO/AEO, content, analytics; for large sites - a developer for markup and speed.

Further: strategy review, step-by-step implementation, contacts.
