What is SKU

SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) is an internal identifier of a product item: “which particular product variant we sell and take into account.” Below is a simple definition, examples and rules on how to make SKU convenient for warehouse, store and analytics.

SKU in simple words - what is it?

SKU is a code that uniquely designates a specific product unit (product variant) in your accounting system: for example, “white T-shirt, size M” is a separate SKU, while “same T-shirt, size L” is a different SKU.

Why do you need a SKU in an online store and in a warehouse?

  • Balance accounting: How many units of each variant are in stock/store.
  • Sales: what exactly is being bought (color/size/equipment) and what brings in the margin.
  • Logistics: order assembly without errors (pick/pack).
  • Returns: it is clear what is being returned and what needs to be returned to the warehouse.
  • Synchronization: linking the catalog with ERP/CRM/marketplaces.

SKU, barcode and article number - what's the difference?

Term What is this Who asks Where is it used?
SKU Internal code of the product item (variant) You (store/warehouse) Accounting, warehouse, analytics, integrations
Barcode (EAN/UPC) Standardized ID for scanning Manufacturer/standard Cash desk, logistics, retail, marketplaces
Article Model/series designation (often from the manufacturer) More often the manufacturer Catalog, prices, documents

One product can have an article number, a barcode, and a SKU. Main: SKU is your internal “key”which you control and you can build it so that it is convenient for your processes.

Example SKUs

  • TSH-WHT-M — T-shirt (TSH), white (WHT), size M.
  • CRM-DENT-12M — CRM subscription for dentistry for 12 months.
  • LEG-BLK-XS-HI — leggings, black, XS, high rise.
  • KIT-STARTER-RU — starter kit (RU localization).

5 rules for a good SKU

  1. Uniqueness: one SKU = one product variant.
  2. Stability: Do not reuse SKU for another product.
  3. Readability: The code should be understandable to people (at least partially).
  4. Limited length: Usually 8–20 characters is enough.
  5. Single format: Same rules for delimiters and abbreviations (for example, hyphens).

FAQ

Does the SKU have to match the barcode?

No. SKU - internal identifier. Barcode is an external standard for scanning. Sometimes they are connected, but they are different entities.

Is it possible to change SKU?

Better to avoid. Changing SKUs often breaks connections in integrations, analytics, and order history. If necessary, do migration with aliases.

Is SKU needed if there are few products?

Yes, if you are planning growth or selling options (size/color/set). SKU disciplines the catalog and reduces errors in orders.
