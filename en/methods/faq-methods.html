FAQ on promotion methods

Short answers to quickly choose an approach: GEO, AEO, SEO, AIO or AI+SMM.

Below are drop-down answers in the same format as the “Frequently Questions” block on the main page. A complete analysis of priorities, method conflicts and a unified system is in the article Promotion methods in 2026. The anchor for the old “summary comparison” links is in the last bullet point.

What is a FAQ on promotion methods?

This is a selection of direct answers about the areas of GEO, AEO, SEO, AIO and AI+SMM: how they differ, where to start, what are the typical errors and how the answer pages are structured. To go deeper, use the guides on individual methods and the review at the link above.

Why is this FAQ needed?

To understand in a few minutes which method or combination solves your problem - citations in neural networks, organics, local scenarios or brand awareness - and do not spend the budget on “everything at once” without a clear priority and measurable artifacts on the site.

What to choose as the first step: GEO, AEO, SEO or AI+SMM?

If citations in AI answers are important, start with AEO (answer pages). If you need stable organic demand, cover basic SEO (accessibility, structure, indexing) at the same time. GEO - when there is geography and service points. AI+SMM - when you need the same facts and wording as on the website to be reinforced in external channels.

Do I need to implement all methods at once?

No. In practice, they take 2-3 areas for the current goal and connect them with content and process: for example, SEO + AEO for organics and answers, plus GEO for a network of salons. The rest is added as response pages, consistent contacts, and clear metrics appear.

How does GEO differ from SEO in practice?
  • SEO — organic traffic by topics and queries, technical base and linking.
  • GEO — local relevance (city, region), maps, “near me” queries, unified NAP data.
  • Intersection — local answer pages, reviews, cards in directories and consistency with the site.
How do you know when it’s time to focus on GEO, and not just SEO?

If a significant part of requests comes from maps and local requests, there are several addresses or an exit zone, and in the search results you compete with “nearby” - without GEO you lose the context of the place. Signs: mobile phones and clocks on maps, no pages for the city/district, few reviews and answers to them.

What is AEO in simple words?

AEO is the design of text so that the neural network can take your paragraph as a ready-made answer: the question is in the title, the essence is in the first sentences, then the steps, table or comparison, limitations and mini-FAQ.

What is an answer page and how is it different from a regular SEO page?

The answer page is tailored to one clear intent of the question: first a direct answer, then details. A classic SEO page often drags down volume and keywords, which is why the “core” of the answer moves down. For AI and quick snippets, the winning format is: H2 as a user question, short answer block, structure below.

Is it possible to strengthen AEO without completely reworking the site?

Yes. Usually they start with high commercial and question URLs: rewrite introductory paragraphs and H2 for the answer, add FAQ and tables, remove contradictions between pages. At the same time, they are fixing things that are critical for indexing in SEO, otherwise new answers simply will not be included in the sample.

What is AIO and how is it different from AEO?

AIO is broader: it includes a response structure (as in AEO), plus a single “fact base” for the brand - cases with numbers, conditions and restrictions, agreed upon entities, internal coherence of sections and structured data logic where appropriate.

Is Schema.org markup required for AIO?

Not as a “magic button”, but as a hint to machines about the type of entity (organization, service, product, FAQ). Useful when the page already has honest content and does not duplicate misleading information. Without clear text, markup does little to help AI respond.

Why is “just being on social media” not enough for AI visibility?

A stable source for citation often remains a website: an article, a case, a service page. SMM can enhance recognition and wording, but it is better to anchor supporting facts on official URLs in the format of answer pages and evidence.

How to measure the effect of promotion in AI responses and neurosearch?

They look at a bunch of signals: an increase in brand mentions in responses to control prompts, transitions marked AI/chat referrals in analytics (where configured), indirectly - an increase in questionable organic traffic and conversions from answer pages. It is important to fix in advance a list of queries and source pages for repeated measurements.

Does contextual advertising replace SEO, AEO and GEO?

No. Advertising provides paid coverage at a rate; SEO and AEO are building an asset that works on organics and the quality of sources for models. GEO adds local truth to maps and reference books. It is optimal to combine: advertising for quick tests of hypotheses, content and structure for sustainable visibility.

What mistakes are most often made when trying to “advance in neural networks”?
  • Long texts without question headings and without a direct answer in the first 2–4 sentences.
  • There are no verifiable facts: numbers, terms, conditions, restrictions - there is nothing to “catch onto” when quoting.
  • Single articles without a cluster and linking around the topic.
  • Broken links and 404 in the menu break trust in the site as a source.
Formula “Conclusion + Condition + Proof” - why is it needed?
  • Conclusion - a clear thesis that can be quoted as a separate line.
  • Condition — when and for whom it is true, what framework (steps, table, FAQ).
  • Proof — numbers, cases, consistent wording in several sections of the site.
Mini-checklist: what should be on the response page?
  1. H1 = topic, H2 = user question.
  2. Short answer in the first paragraph (guideline 60–120 words).
  3. List of steps or table of selection factors.
  4. Block “when it doesn’t fit” or restrictions.
  5. 3–6 clarifying questions at the bottom of the page.
