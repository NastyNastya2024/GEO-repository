What are neuroplatforms

Definition, RAG and the role of external data; overview of the global AI platform market and key LLMs; then a practical comparison section products (ChatGPT, Perplexity, DeepSeek, Claude, Google Gemini, Yandex Alice) for GEO, AEO and SEO.

What is a neuroplatform?

Neuroplatform is a software system based on a large language model that can conduct a dialogue with the user, understand requests and generate responses in different formats (text, code, images, etc.). In fact, this is not just a model, but a combination of the neural network itself, an interaction interface and a layer for working with external data and services.

An example of such a system is ChatGPT, where the language model is combined with search tools, file analysis and additional functions.

The key mechanism of modern neural platforms is RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation). This is an approach in which the system first finds relevant information in external sources (for example, the Internet or a document database), and then uses it as context to generate a response. Thus, the model does not respond only on the basis of pre-trained knowledge, but “pulls up” current data in real time.

As a result, the neuroplatform is combination of generation and search, where RAG ensures the relevance and accuracy of answers by connecting external data to the model's operation process.

1. What is considered a “neuroplatform”?

In industrial analytics, this is usually understood as:

  • LLM platforms — chat AI and assistants with a user interface;
  • foundation models — text, code, multimodal models as a product base;
  • AI-cloud platforms — API and calculations (inference) for third-party applications;
  • vertical AI systems — search, coding, design, analytics and other narrow scenarios.

What “search engines” are LLMs trained on - and what is the typical mistake here?

This is a critical point that is often misrepresented: LLMs are not “search engine trained” literally like the Google button. In practice, the linkage is used pre-training at corporations, additional training и retrieval (search/index) is already in the product.

Where does the data come from during training and in production?

Type Examples
Web crawl Common Crawl, C4, RefinedWeb, etc.
Corporate/Closed Indices Google - own index; Baidu - Baidu search index; Meta - internal data and access policies
Licensed Data News (AP, Axel Springer, etc.), books, scientific databases
User-generated Reddit, Stack Overflow, GitHub (in different models and years - in different ways and with different policies)

Architecturally it is convenient to represent it like this: LLM = pretraining (web, etc.) + fine-tuning + retrieval (search index / corporate database). It is important for GEO/AEO practice to separate two layers of influence:

  • Model training - affects the “understanding” of language, tasks and typical formulations;
  • Retrieval - influences what documents fall into context response to the user.

Exactly retrieval closer to the “new SEO”: without indexed, structured and trusted pages, it is more difficult to get into the subset of sources from which the platform collects the answer.
