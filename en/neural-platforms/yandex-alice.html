Promotion in Yandex Alice

Alice is a “response” channel: the winners are pages where the question is formulated like the user’s, and the answer is given immediately and briefly. In 2026, this is especially important for voice queries and local scenarios.

What is Yandex Alice

Yandex is a Russian IT ecosystem that includes search, maps, taxis, marketplaces and other services (though the legal structure and geography may differ depending on the product). Alice is the voice and text assistant of this ecosystem. It is built into the Yandex application, smart speakers, navigator and partner devices.

Alice's answers are formed on the basis of Yandex services - primarily Search, Maps and directories. Therefore, for businesses in Russia, Alice is actually a continuation of search results and local search, and not a separate global chat without reference to sources. Which sites and cards are shown or spoken is determined by algorithms within the Yandex ecosystem.

Where does Alice get companies and places to answer from?

Alice constructs answers in conjunction with Yandex Search, Maps, directories and scenarios of the Yandex ecosystem: for queries “where to buy”, “nearby”, “who is best in the city” the same signals are important as for organic search results and local cards (relevance, NAP, reviews, website, hours). The company is voiced or shown if it is confidently present in this circuit. Unlike ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, this is not a “global LLM without reference to one search engine”, but Russian search stack; unlike Gemini, there is no Google index and Alphabet maps - only Yandex and partner sources that the product connects. The rules for display and skills are changing - focus on the latest Yandex materials for webmasters and businesses.

Why is it important for a brand to be included in Alice’s answers?

The answer is spoken aloud or shown on the screen - the user can go straight to maps, website or call. For local services, retail and HoReCa, this is a “first touch” channel, parallel to the classic search in the Yandex line.

How to get into Yandex Alice's answers in 2026?

Make answer pages for voice intents: “how much does it cost…”, “how to get there…”, “near me…”. In each block, use question-H2 and answer-first paragraph, then a list of steps/conditions and FAQ.

What queries are most often “voice”?

  • “Where to buy nearby...”, “how to get there...”, “what are the opening hours...”.
  • “How much does it cost...”, “how to choose...”, “what is the guarantee...”.
  • “Best... in [city]”, “top... near me.”

FAQ: Yandex Alice and voice responses

What is the best answer length for Alice?

1–2 sentences (approximately 30–60 words) immediately below the H2 question. Then you can reveal the details in a list, but the first fragment should sound like a ready answer.

Which pages are most often used in voice scripts?

Pages with specifics: “prices”, “schedule”, “how to get there”, “conditions”, “guarantees”, “near me”, “in [city/district]”. It is important that local facts are consistent and repeated without contradiction.

What is more important: SEO text or local facts?

For Alice, local attributes and short answers are more important. Long paragraphs without facts are less well spoken and less well understood.

Do you need a FAQ for a voice assistant?

Yes. An FAQ with 5–8 questions helps to match user wording (“how much”, “where”, “how”, “when”), and short answers can be quoted/voiced.

How to check that a page is actually “voiced”?

Go through the page like a script: each H2 is a question, the first paragraph is a short answer. If this paragraph sounds naturally spoken and contains facts (address/time/conditions), you are close to ideal.
