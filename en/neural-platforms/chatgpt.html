What is ChatGPT

What is ChatGPT

OpenAI (USA) is a company that develops generative AI. ChatGPT — its main product in the model-based chat format GPT: It works with text and, if necessary, with voice and files. If search and additional tools are enabled, answers are generated taking into account current information from the Internet and downloaded data. If not, the model’s knowledge at the time of training and the context of the current dialogue are used. The set of available functions depends on the OpenAI policy, region and selected tariff.

Where does ChatGPT get companies and sites to respond to?

Recommendations and lists of brands in the response are not “manually hard-coded”: the model collects text from the context available to it - when enabled web search this is a selection of pages and snippets from search partner (which engine and how the results are generated depends on the product and region), when turned off - mostly training data and current dialogue. The company name appears if it appears in the extracted fragments or is consistently found in the knowledge at the training date. Unlike Perplexity,The interface is less likely to emphasize an explicit list of ,sources; unlike Gemini, there is no built-in connection with the Google index and maps by default; unlike Yandex Alice, relying not on Yandex search results and cards, but on the global web and chat settings. The operator's retrieval pipeline is evolving - check the behavior against the latest OpenAI help.

Why is it important for a brand to be included in ChatGPT responses?

The user often perceives the answer as a ready-made recommendation or a brief overview of the market: The brand may be named directly, indirectly (in a list of options), or not named if there are no clear facts about you in the available sources. Landing on “highly retrieved” pages and external mentions increases the chance that you will be more likely to be matched to the answer in connected searches and typical category questions—this complements classic SEO and advertising.

What does ChatGPT rely on when collecting a response?

The system does not have a “favorite site” in the sense of conventional SEO ranking. She picks up pieces of knowledge that look like a good ready-made answer: so that the title and beginning of the text coincide with the wording of the question, the essence is clear from the first lines, there are numbers, terms and conditions, and not just general words. Honest reservations (“when it won’t work”) and examples are especially appreciated: before/after, steps, comparison of options.

Frequently asked questions: what kind of “system” is it, where does it get sites from and what is more important

What is a “system” and how does it “think”?

By “system” we mean a combination of a neural network (a large language model) and the environment in which it works: sometimes this is just training data and your request, sometimes - plus a web search or attached documents. She does not “love” the site and does not store a personal top, like classic search results for each keyword: she selects fragments of text that, in terms of meaning, best fit with the wording of the question and look like a coherent, ready-made answer. She does not know how to “think” in the everyday sense - this means predicting the most appropriate words and relying on pieces of knowledge found or laid down during training.

How does the system find sites and through what sources does it search?

Depends on the ChatGPT mode (whether there is web search, plugins, downloaded files, corporate GPT with a fixed base). In search mode, the answer is based on the results of the search partner: the model receives a selection of pages and snippets, and does not “crawl the Internet” itself, like a person in a browser. Without a fresh search, what remains is a snapshot of knowledge at the time of training and your dialogue - then your specific site may not appear at all if it was not in the data or it was not included in the context. Therefore, both clear pages on the domain and what can be found about you on the open web are important.

How will the system show exactly my site in the response?

There are no guarantees: the model may retell the essence without a link, connect several sources, or not name the domain. The chance is higher if your page is included in the search results, at the beginning there is a direct answer to the same question, there are numbers and conditions, and the URL and title clearly correspond to the topic. The interface can show links or a “sources” block - these are product and search quality rules, and not “assigning a site to a position,” as in conventional SEO.

What is better - to develop your website or articles on local sites and in directories?

Not competition, but connection. The site is convenient as canonical source: services, prices, geography, contacts, cases, answer pages - all in one place under your domain. Local and industry platforms, maps, directories, reviews and media provide the essence of “who you are and where to find you” and external confirmation. If numbers and addresses diverge between the site and, for example, maps, It’s harder for the “system” and people to trust the answer. Practical order: align the facts everywhere, on the site - full pages of answers; on external sites - the same wording and links to official URLs, where appropriate.

Why did the system name the site in one chat, but retell it without a link in another?

The answer depends on the wording of the question, whether web search is enabled, what exactly is included in the context (your dialogue, search results, length limits), and on the variability of the model itself. It didn’t “break” or “the site was banned” - the set of fragments on which the system relied changed. Therefore, it is useful to check several wordings and repeat the check over time, rather than focusing on one successful screenshot.

What is more important for the answer: what the model “remembered” during training, or a fresh search on the Internet?

For current prices, regulations and news without searching, the model may be incorrect or out of date. For general explanations of “how it works,” generalized knowledge is often enough. It is safer for business to assume that verifiable facts should live on your website and in updated open sources - then both when searching and when relying on the model’s memory it is easier not to contradict yourself.

Is it possible to “force” the system to always show only my domain?

There is no reliable way like in the advertising bid: there is no guaranteed pinning of “only our URL”. You can increase your likelihood through the quality of your response pages, brand recognition, consistency of facts, and whether that for targeted queries, it is your URLs that end up in search results that connect to the chat. For closed scenarios, they use their own GPT with an attached database - this is a different product and a different audience.

A consistent description of the company, services and contacts on the website and in open sources is also important: this makes it easier to understand who we are talking about and not to confuse you with a similar name. Further in the article there are specific steps and types of questions; In checklists, marketers often say “one meaning - one page address” - this is precisely about having its own clear page for each type of question.

How to get into ChatGPT answers in 2026?

Make separate pages for frequently asked questions from clients: each has one main question in the title and a direct answer at the very beginning (answer-first), then a short summary (TL;DR), tables or step-by-step lists, and a mini-FAQ. Strengthen trust: cases with numbers and deadlines, external references, identical contacts and brand descriptions wherever they can be verified.

There are no guarantees. Neither the page design, nor the cases, nor a single NAP are a “pass” to the ChatGPT response in the sense of an agreement with the service or a fixed algorithm. It's about increasing probabilitythat when selecting fragments, the system will select your text as convenient, verifiable and consistent - especially when a web search is connected to the dialogue and real URLs are included in the context.

  1. 10 pages of answers to the most important questions from the audience.
  2. 3 cases with numbers and deadlines.
  3. Related pages and comparisons "A vs B".
  4. Check: Ask the same questions in ChatGPT every two weeks and see if you and your pages appear in the answer or among the sources.

“System” in one paragraph: site layer and brand layer

For practice, it is convenient to separate the two types of signals. Site layer — this is structured information on your domain: answer pages for specific wording of questions, internal linking, technical accessibility for robots (indexing, speed, absence of duplicates without canonical), obvious facts (prices, terms, geography, conditions) and proof pages (cases, comparisons). This is a “knowledge library” from which the model or search layer can pull a completed paragraph.

Brand Layer on the Open Internet - everything by which the system and people understand who are you and is it possible to check the facts: cards on maps, directories, social networks, reviews, media, partner pages, profiles in industry directories. If there are different phone numbers, names of services or prices than on the website, trust in the “official” version drops - the model more often generalizes, is cautious, or refers to someone else’s source. The ideal picture: the site sets the canon (what is considered true), external sites confirm it, and do not argue with it.

What else is usually needed in addition to the checklist: pages must actually be opened and indexed; user requests in the chat should at least vaguely coincide with how the question is formulated in your H2/H1; do not rely on one “main” landing page for all topics; monitor obsolescence (prices and regulations); if you have a web search, understand that classic search results also come into play as an intermediate stage (if your URL doesn’t get there, it may not get into the chat context).

Questions after the checklist: guarantees, completeness of requirements, TOP 10

I’ll create answer pages and cases - will this guarantee that I’ll be included in the ChatGPT answers?

No. This is the best "reduce the risk of invisibility and increase the chance of citation" strategy available without access to OpenAI's internal systems, but does not promise results. The answer depends on the mode (search is enabled or not), the language and wording of the question, the competition of the topic, what is included in the results, context limits and model variability. It makes sense to measure not just “hit/miss”, but the dynamics according to the checklist of questions and the appearance of your domain among sources.

Give a full description: what exactly should be in the “information system” on the site and about the brand?

On the website (minimum of a meaningful outline): one page - one main user question; in the first paragraphs there is a direct answer; further structure (steps, table, comparison); block “when it doesn’t fit”; mini-FAQ on the same topic; linking to adjacent answers; contacts and geography where this is part of the answer; cases with metrics and deadlines; single glossary (same names of products and services). Technique: correct response codes, mobile readability, clear URLs, without massive duplicates that blur the meaning of the page.

About the brand outside the site: agreed legal entity/brand name, address, phone number, hours, work area - in maps and directories as on the website; profiles on social networks without conflicting promotions and “old” prices; reviews and responses to them where your audience sees them; external references (media, partners) that do not refute the official page. The purpose of the brand layer is to ensure that when comparing sources there are no “two truths.”

Is there a correlation between “TOP 10 in Google/Yandex” and “TOP 10 in AI search results”?

There is neither a fixed odd nor a mirror TOP-10. Classic SEO rating is based on search engine results based on many factors; the ChatGPT response is assembled differently: from fragments relevant to the wording of the question in the dialogue, plus, when the search is turned on, from what the search layer gave as candidates. Therefore, a high position in organics Maybe help (the page is more often included in the selection), but does not guarantee a quote and does not equal a “place in AI”. The opposite is also true: a page may rarely be “number one” in the classics, but it is good as a compact answer to a narrow question. It makes sense to compare trends and intersections across a list of URLs, rather than line-to-line positions.

What errors prevent citation?

  • One landing page instead of a URL library.
  • No direct answers or TL;DR.
  • There are no restrictions and no evidence.

FAQ: ChatGPT and citations

Do I need to make separate pages for each question?

Yes. It’s most convenient when each type of question (“how to do”, “what to choose”, “how much does it cost”) has its own page with one main meaning - in professional terminology this is called “one intent - one URL”: it is easier for the system to find exactly the document that is needed for the answer, without mixing different topics on one page.

What is the optimal length of a “short answer”?

40–120 words: enough for the answer to be complete, but still remain a fragment that can be quoted. Next, add the table/steps and restrictions.

What is more important: tables or cases?

Tables increase retrieval (criteria/comparisons), and cases increase credibility (numbers/terms/conditions). Ideally on key pages should have both: a selection table and 1-2 supporting examples.

How to increase the chance of a brand being mentioned in a response?

Give stable “essences”: the same positioning, 3-5 repeatable points, agreed upon facts, plus separate pages “how we work”, cases and experts.

What errors most often “break” citations?

General phrases without numbers and conditions, no “when it doesn’t fit” clauses, several unrelated questions on one page and weak links between explanation, comparison, case study and FAQ - then it is difficult for either a person or a system to pull out one clear passage for a quote.
