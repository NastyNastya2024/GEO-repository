What is DeepSeek

DeepSeek — Chinese AI company and product of the same name: own large language models, web chat и API for retelling, coding, studying, comparing and editing texts; some versions come out with open scales, and the competitive price of the API makes the service popular among developers. It is important for businesses to remember: this is a commercial operator from China - terms of use, data and API availability you need to check only with the current policy DeepSeek and agreement.

Where does DeepSeek get companies and sites to answer from?

DeepSeek user chat combines generation with the ability to rely on fresh web in those modes where search is enabled (implementation depends on the client and version): the model does not index the Internet itself, but receives subset of pages and snippets, which were transmitted by the search/retriever layer. Brand names and “who to recommend” often come from reviews, comparisons, feature tables and tech media. Unlike Perplexity, the emphasis on a constant block of external links in each answer is less predictable; unlike Gemini, no connection with Google index; unlike Alice, no outline Yandex and local cards. The jurisdiction and data policy of the operator (PRC) differ from Western products - take this into account in compliance.

Why is it important for brands to consider DeepSeek?

Some of your audience may receive product comparisons, selections and explanations namely through DeepSeek (especially in price-sensitive and tech segments). If there are no clear answer pages and external confirmations about the brand, the model will often generalize “as is the market average” without your name.

How to write headers so that DeepSeek finds a page when requested?

Make H2 in the form of a real request: “How to set up GEO for an online store in 2026?”, “How to measure citations in neural networks?” Immediately below H2 is a short answer, then a list and a table.

For DeepSeek, the coincidence of the wording of the query and the H2 question, as well as the “retrievability” of the answer, is critical: short paragraph, table/steps, limitations and evidence. Then the page becomes a response document for retrieval.

What page structure is best for DeepSeek to read?

  1. H2‑question → 1 paragraph answer.
  2. TL;DR list.
  3. Table of factors/criteria.
  4. Limitations + examples.
  5. FAQ + related pages.

Why does “Conclusion + Condition + Evidence” help ranking?

Conclusion: structured statements are easier to cite.

Condition: if you add constraints and application context.

Proof: cases, figures, tables and verifiable facts.

FAQ: DeepSeek and structured answers

What headlines does DeepSeek understand best?

Those that repeat the actual wording of the user’s request: “how...”, “what...”, “why...”, “A vs B”. You need a short answer right under the title, and then the steps/table.

Do we need to add restrictions and “when it doesn’t fit”?

Yes. Constraints reduce ambiguity and make the answer precise. This increases the chance that the AI will quote your fragment, rather than “collecting” the answer from different sources.

What is more important: long text or structural blocks?

Structure. DeepSeek wins on “mined” blocks: short answer paragraph, table of criteria/factors, list of steps, FAQ, then evidence (case/figures).

Which page layout gives the best effect?

H1 → short lead → TOC → series of H2 questions → answer-first paragraphs → tables/steps → restrictions → FAQ → “Read also.” On this site, some blocks are added automatically.

How often do you update content for AI search?

At least once every 1-2 months for key pages: update dates, examples, numbers, tools and internal links. This maintains relevance and increases the credibility of the document.
