Where does Claude get companies and documents to answer?

Claude is strong at long context and in working with files that a user or organization connects (projects, artifacts, corporate databases - according to tariff and settings). When available web, the retrieved pages are added to the context through the search layer, but the typical B2B scenario is not a “random search engine”, but a combination of downloaded documents and access policy. The company name is included in the response if it is clearly included in attachments or in fragments received from the network. Unlike Perplexity, this is not a link aggregator product for every public request; unlike Gemini, no Google Search and Maps ecosystem; unlike Alice, there is no voice contour and Yandex “near me” issuance. For actual modes (web, connectors), please check the current Anthropic terms and conditions.

Why is it important for a brand to consider Claude?

In corporate scenarios, Claude may participate in preparing market summaries, supplier comparisons and draft reports. If there are no structured facts about your company on the open web, the answer will often be from the big players in the category.

How does Claude “choose” what is considered a good source?

Claude makes better use of materials that are easy to retrieve and retell: question headings, precise definitions, criteria tables, and a “when not appropriate” section. The less ambiguity and more verifiable facts, the higher the chance that your text will become a “ready answer.”

For Claude, pages with precise definitions, selection criteria, and clear limitations work best. The fewer contradictions and the more verifiable attributes (numbers, terms, conditions), the higher the chance of becoming a “source”.

Which pages work best for AI citations?

  • Definition pages (“What is…?”) with a short TL;DR.
  • How-to pages (“How to do ...?”) with steps and checklist.
  • Comparisons (“… vs …”) with difference table and selection scenarios.
  • Examples/cases with numbers, terms and conditions.

What trust signals need to be added to make AI name the brand more confidently?

  • Cases with “before/after” metrics.
  • Experts: “who does” page + experience + publications.
  • Repeatable facts about the product: 3-5 points without contradictions.
  • External mentions and reviews (guest articles, directories, ratings).

Formula “Conclusion + Condition + Proof” for pages under Claude

  • Conclusion: Claude is better at repeating structured answers.
  • Condition: If the page has tables/steps and restrictions.
  • Proof: The same thesis is confirmed by the case and repeated in the FAQ/comparison.

Mini-checklist: what to do in 7 days

  1. 10 H2 questions for audience requests.
  2. 10 short answers (60–120 words) + selection criteria table.
  3. 2 comparisons (vs) + 1 case with numbers.
  4. Linking: methods → comparisons → cases → FAQ.

FAQ: Claude and citation

What does Claude quote most readily?

Short direct answers under H2 questions, then criteria/table, limitations and supporting facts (case/figures). The easier it is to retrieve a “ready quote,” the higher the chance that it will end up in a response.

Should I write “when it doesn’t fit”?

Yes. The “when it doesn’t fit” block reduces the risk of an incorrect recommendation and makes the document more accurate - this increases trust and likelihood of being used as a source.

Which trust signals are more important: the author or the cases?

They work together: the author/expert explains “why to believe”, and the cases prove “that it works”. On key pages it is advisable to have both an examination block and 1–2 evidentiary examples.

How to avoid contradictions in terminology?

Record 3-5 definitions and theses and repeat them equally on all pages of the cluster. If the wording changes, update related pages at the same time so that there are no “two versions of the truth.”

Do I need to make separate pages for intents?

Yes: “what is it”, “how to do it”, “comparison”, “cases”, “FAQ”. Тогда Claude (и другие answer engines) проще извлекают нужный document for a specific question.
