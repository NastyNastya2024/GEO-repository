Case: marketplaces

How AI makes the selection of “what to buy”: attributes, evidence, comparisons and clear limitations.

What methods are a priority and a typical strategy

Under SEO taxonomies and filters What is meant here is not the “magic of external links”, but clear directory structure on the site itself (and on your website, if there is one next to the storefront): nesting of categories, common attribute names, meaningful filters in the left column or in the results, CNC and stable pages for “category + characteristic” combinations. Both the classic search on the site/marketplace and how easy it is for neural networks and search engines to “collect” the answer from cards and guides depend on this. AEO — on selection pages: attributes, comparisons, Q&A, reviews in the “question-short answer” format. AIO — when the conditions of the seller, delivery and return are described uniformly and without contradictions between the card, certificate and marketing. GEO — for multicity logistics and local services. AI+SMM — so that the promo and “brand voice” do not diverge from the facts on the window.

Typical strategy: align the structure of categories and cards → “how to choose” and comparison pages → a single block of conditions and restrictions → when scaling, local signals for delivery and pick-up points. Summary of the order of methods - in review of promotion methods; close in meaning - e-commerce cases.

Frequently asked questions: where is SEO done, social media, metadata, SKU or brand

What are SEO taxonomies and filters - is it inside the marketplace or on external links?

First of all inside the showcase that the user sees and indexes the search: category tree, characteristics in the card, filters (size, brand, compatibility, etc.), the same names of the same feature in all products. External links and mentions on other sites increase trust and brand recognition, but do not replace the clutter in the catalog: if the filters are crooked and the attributes are empty, neither the link nor the post will “gather” this into a clear answer for AI. If the brand has your website, the same logic is transferred there: the same terminology, the same attribute values, linking “guide → selection → SKU”.

What role do social networks play for the marketplace and the seller?

Social networks - about demand, explanation of choice and recognition: reviews, short answers to typical questions, promotions, working with comments and UGC. For AI visibility, it is important that numbers, configurations, delivery times and promotion restrictions coincided with a card and certificate at the site; otherwise, the model sees two versions of the truth and is cautious. Social networks are rarely the only “canon” - the showcase and/or official website with the same wording remains the mainstay.

Where should the metadata be (title, description, markup)?

On every significant page of the showcase, which you want to find in searches and from which you can quote: category, filtered results (if the platform provides a separate URL), product card, “how to choose” guide, delivery/return policy page, seller’s brand showcase. Unique title and the description is the essence of the page; structured data (for example, Product, Offer, FAQ where there is a question-answer block) - in the HTML of the same page according to the rules of the site or your template. You cannot duplicate conflicting metas on different URLs: search and AI compare snippets with the body of the page.

Are they promoting a single SKU on the website or an entire brand?

Usually both at different levels. SKU/card — for queries “which product”, “compatibility with...”, “what’s included”. Brand and line - for “who is it suitable for”, “what makes the series different”, trust and repeat purchases. A convenient combination for AI is: brand/category guide with selection criteria → selections/SKUs with the same attributes and cross-references. A single “ribbon” site with no structure is worse than fewer URLs but with a clear hierarchy.

Which social networks should you post content on?

Not in “everyone”, but where your audience already exists and the product format is natural: visual categories - sites with photos/videos; B2B or complex product - professional communities and long content where people read it. Regularity, the coincidence of facts with the showcase and the presence of links to official source pages are more important than the number of sites. For international brands, there are often separate accounts by region - then a single glossary and consistent conditions across markets are even more important.

What examples to follow (non-Russian brands)

  • Amazon: attributes, comparisons, Q&A, reviews, options/modifications.
  • eBay: condition/compatibility/limitations, seller's trust.
  • Etsy: brand/maker history, materials, sizes, UGC.

What does it take for a product to be included in AI selections?

  1. Attributes and Entities: material, size, compatibility, purpose, limitations.
  2. Comparisons: table “why this option”, alternatives and selection criteria.
  3. Proof: reviews, photos of customers, answers to questions.
  4. Politicians: delivery, returns, warranty - short answers.

Content Architecture

  • Categories with guide selection blocks (“how to choose”) and FAQ.
  • Cards with “answer‑first” description + characteristics table.
  • Comparisons “X vs Y” (brands/models) at the category level.

Technical signals

  • Schema: Product, Offer, AggregateRating, Review, FAQPage.
  • A unified dictionary of attributes (we call the same thing the same).
  • Internal linking: category → guide → selections → cards.
