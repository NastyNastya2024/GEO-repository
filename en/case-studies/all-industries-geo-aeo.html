What do a marketplace and legal advice have in common?

The neural network does not divide the world into “our industries from the list” and “the rest.” She matches the request, context and what can be learned about a brand, service or product from open sources: website, cards, directories, media, reviews, structured data, knowledge bases. If there are few signals or they are contradictory, the brand is less likely to receive a response or inferior to those who are more clearly described online.

Therefore, the task of GEO (visibility in generative output) and AEO (readiness for answers and citations) not “about a five-point niche”, but about clarity of essence: who are you, what do you offer, where you operate, what supports your claims, and how this relates to typical audience questions.

Which methods are a priority depends on the type of business

There is no universal order: local networks are more important GEO and contact consistency, SaaS - response page clusters and proofs (AEO/AIO), online stores - SEO catalog and selection guides, strong brands with a sparse website - alignment of facts between channels (AI+SMM + AIO). Why industry scenarios on the site are not a limitation

Case studies like marketplaces, clinics, or delivery are convenient as educational examples: they are easy to show entities (product, service, zone, branch), attributes and standard queries. The same scheme is transferred to B2B manufacturing, education, construction, logistics, IT services, etc. Vocabulary changes and a set of fields, but not a principle: the model looks for confirmed facts and coherent explanations, and not “a beautiful slogan without support.”

What to check in your industry

  1. Brand Essence: single name, description, geography, contacts - no duplicates and outdated cards.
  2. Answers to typical questions: price/conditions, terms, restrictions, comparisons - short and to the point, no fluff.
  3. Proof: cases, reviews, certificates, publications - what reduces uncertainty for the model and the person.
  4. Structure and layout: headings, lists, tables, FAQ, schema.org wherever it is appropriate - so that the answers are easier to collect and quote.
  5. Relevance: Outdated pages and contradictions between sources undermine trust and visibility in AI responses just like in classic SEO.
