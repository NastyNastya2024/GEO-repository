Case: beauty (beauty salons)

How to get into AI selections “where to sign up” and “which procedure to choose”: clear services, contraindications and evidence.

What methods are a priority and a typical strategy

Salons and beauty retail usually need GEO (location, recording, hours), AEO on services and products (“who is suitable”, stages of the procedure, care after the visit) and AIO for consistent descriptions of formulations, contraindications, and recording policies. SEO maintains service structure and blog/guides. AI+SMM critical with strong UGC: reviews and posts must be based on the same wording as the site, otherwise the models mix up the versions.

Typical strategy: cards of salons and masters → price list and packages in answer format → guides to procedures → alignment of descriptions on social networks and marketplaces with the official website. The order of the methods is in review of promotion methods.

What examples to follow (non-Russian brands)

  • Sephora: selection guides, reviews, UGC, answers to questions about application.
  • Ulta Beauty: taxonomy of services/products, local pages, posts.
  • Glossier: simple explanations, ingredients, “who is suitable/not suitable”.

What AI is trying to understand in beauty

  • Which service/procedure suits the client’s task and limitations.
  • How safe is it (contraindications, after care).
  • Why you can trust this brand/salon (experience, cases, reviews).

Pages that provide recommendations

  1. Service pages: effect, steps, duration, price/range, contraindications, after care.
  2. Profiles of masters: experience, specialization, certificates, examples of work.
  3. Selection guides: “what to choose”, “what is different”, tables of criteria.
  4. Local pages: address, entry, reviews, photos, routes.

Proof

  • Before/after photos with clear conditions (no manipulation).
  • Reviews + answers + aggregators.
  • FAQ on risks/limitations - honest and short.

Technical signals

  • Schema: LocalBusiness, Service, Person (masters), FAQPage, Review.
  • Unified terminology of procedures and ingredients (essences).
  • Relinking: “service → master → branch → record.”
