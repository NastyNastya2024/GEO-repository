AI‑discovery cases: how brands use GEO/AEO

A selection of “clean” examples: brands that build answer-first content, trust signals and structure, which the AI easily retrieves and repeats.

What methods are a priority and a typical strategy

Leading brands usually have AI discovery that is both strong AEO и AIO: answer-first pages, comparisons, FAQs and repeatable facts that the model can quote without distortion. SEO gives the scale and connectivity of clusters; GEO added at offline points and local services. AI+SMM and PR consolidate one narrative with the site - otherwise, different “versions” of the brand are mixed in the responses.

A typical leader strategy: system layer “intent → URL → proof” → regularly updating answer pages → measuring AI referral traffic. The general order of methods is in review of promotion methods.

Which cases are considered “clean” for GEO/AEO/AI‑discovery?

Those where you can see not a “post about AI”, but a system layer: definition pages, comparisons, FAQ architecture, strong linking, proof of trust and measurement of AI‑referral traffic.

HubSpot: Why are they often considered the standard for answer-first strategy?

  • “Direct answer” format pages (definition/what is/how to/vs).
  • FAQ structure and listicles/comparison pages.
  • Content that the LLM can cite in a paragraph.
  • Focus on measuring AI referral traffic and conversion.

Semrush: how do they do “ownership of category” in AI search?

They don’t just write “about SEO”, but capture demand for a new category: AI Overviews / AI visibility / AEO / AI search - and build content and analytics around it.

  • Content “how to get into AI replies” and “how to track AI mentions”.
  • Research: what types of pages are more likely to be included in AI Overviews.
  • Products and metrics around AI visibility.

Ahrefs: What works for them for AI citations?

  • Definition pages and glossary‑style content.
  • Comparison content under the intent “vs / alternatives”.
  • Strong internal linking and clear semantic structure.
  • Strategy: Be the underlying source of knowledge that AI references.

Go Fish Digital: what lesson does their micro-case for ChatGPT Search teach?

AI often ranks not by “SEO position,” but by brand clarity and trust attributes. They strengthened the layer of trust, so that the AI does not “think of it”, but confidently names the company.

  • Notable clients
  • Awards
  • Case studies
  • Service specialization

Prerender.io: why does GEO start with crawlability and not text?

If the AI crawler cannot read the page due to HTML rendering/availability, content marketing is not will help. Their focus is renderability, HTML accessibility and the removal of JS barriers.

TUI: why is the travel niche especially strong for GEO and conversational discovery?

Travel queries are often formulated as ready-made script questions: “where to go”, “best hotels”, “route to 5 days.” The winner is the one who packages the answer as a finished fragment.

  • Destination pages
  • Itinerary pages
  • Travel FAQ
  • Comparison content
  • Intent‑based pages (family/all‑inclusive/city break/honeymoon)

Booking.com: What makes their catalog a “machine-readable unit of recommendation”?

  • The most structured catalog and long-tail intent pages.
  • UGC/reviews as a source of trust.
  • AI-compatible attributes (“near center”, “great breakfast”, “best for couples”).

Airbnb: How do they package the product for the user scenario?

  • Strong category pages and curated landing pages.
  • Semantic grouping of objects and scenarios (romantic weekend/cabins/remote work).
  • UGC + reviews + host authority as evidence.

Expedia: Why should a brand become an “answer tool” rather than a link?

Behavior is shifting: “I don’t want 20 links - I want the best option.” Expedia is building conversational trip planning and AI‑guided discovery, turning the request into itinerary‑format.

Amazon: why is it not the card that wins AI shopping, but the response card?

  • Product detail pages that answer questions.
  • Q&A and reviews with extracted attributes.
  • Comparison intent: best/vs/alternatives/for beginners.

Walmart: what is the “product knowledge layer” for AI‑discovery?

In an AI search, the winner is not the one who has “a lot of products,” but the one whose products are better described for the machine to answer: attributes, structures, intent pages, easy retrieval.

Sephora: why is beauty one of the most LLM‑friendly niches?

  • Intent-driven content (“best for oily skin”, “routine for acne”).
  • Answer-led approach and buying guides.
  • Reviews + use-case content that is easy to summarize.

Nike: how “use-case landing pages” help AI recommend a brand?

AI better recommends a brand that has structured the product in advance for the scenario: “best running shoes for...” and “gym shoes vs running shoes”.

L'Oréal: what is important for AI responses in the “need → solution” niche?

  • Expert content and ingredient explainers.
  • Routine pages and recommendation paths.
  • Semantics around concerns (dryness/sensitivity/acne).

Mayo Clinic: why is it almost the standard of AEO architecture?

Structure “definition → symptoms → causes → diagnosis → treatment → when to see a doctor” plus high the level of trust makes the content highly cited.

Cleveland Clinic: Why does their content act as a canned answer database?

  • Condition pages, symptom pages, treatment explainers.
  • FAQ approach and strong expertise.
  • Sections that AI can easily retrieve and retell.

NerdWallet: What patterns make financial answers highly cited?

  • Comparison-first content and decision support pages.
  • Calculators + “short answer”.
  • Tables, definitions and FAQ.

Bankrate: Why does AI love “what is the best...” pages?

Because such pages are easy to summarize and quote: tables, rate pages, precise questions, structured recommendations.

Healthline: how to combine simplicity, structure and trust?

  • Structured answer format
  • Expert review
  • FAQ‑logic and topic authority clusters

CN2U.AI: why is this already “the next stage of GEO”?

This is a case where AI becomes a sales channel: AI catalog search, AI recommendations, product improvement discovery and CTR growth thanks to AI‑assisted shopping journey.

What is repeated in all cases?

Pattern Examples from cases
Answer-first structure HubSpot, Mayo Clinic, NerdWallet
Trust (reviews/cases/awards) Go Fish Digital, Booking, Amazon

These patterns work because they reflect retrieval logic: the neural network searches for a document with an intent, then extracts the quoted fragment and checks the credibility of the examples/attributes. Therefore, “question → short answer → table/steps → restrictions → proof” almost always wins.
