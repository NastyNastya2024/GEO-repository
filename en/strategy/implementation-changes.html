Implementation: what changes are we implementing?

Changes can be different: social networks, articles and changes in the product. The goal is to make the brand “understandable” for AI and convenient for choice: criteria, facts, evidence, structure of answers.

1) Social networks: when it works

  • Need to pin topics: product scenarios, typical questions, case studies.
  • Need “social proof”: reviews, UGC, demonstration of use.
  • Need to quickly test messages: wording that is then transferred to the site/articles.

Important: social networks without a “base” (website/product pages) rarely provide a stable effect in AI recommendations.

2) Articles: what exactly we do

  • Answer‑first: the answer is in the first 2–4 lines, followed by an explanation.
  • Criteria tables: “how to choose” and “comparison of options” (this is LLM’s favorite format).
  • FAQ: questions that are actually asked (and that the AI ​​“picks up”).
  • Proof: numbers, examples, cases, sources.
  • Relinking: Link articles to techniques, tools, and product pages.

3) Changes in the digital product code (AEO/GEO)

“About code” is not only about speed. It's structure, accessibility, and machine-readable entities that help AI get it right understand the product, features, limitations and selection rules.

What do we usually change in markup and structure?

  • Entities on the page: explicit names, versions, prices, regions, conditions, restrictions.
  • Common terms: so that AI does not confuse the same thing with different words on different pages.
  • Components of “quick replies”: “short answer” block, steps, criteria, examples.
  • FAQ + structured data: When appropriate.
  • Internal links: “what to read next”, “how to compare”, “how to implement”.

Quality and Availability

  • Speed & UX: The page should open quickly and be readable.
  • Availability: hierarchical headings, alt texts, correct links.
  • Canonical/meta: so that AI/search understands the “main” version of the page.
