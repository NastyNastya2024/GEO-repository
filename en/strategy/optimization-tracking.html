Optimization: tracking the result

Optimization is control of the effect after implementation. AI signals do not change instantly: it takes time, repeated measurements and the right metrics.

What exactly are we tracking?

  • Share of voice: how often AI recommends you vs competitors for the same prompts.
  • Citation: Which URLs and brands does the AI ​​cite as sources?
  • Scenarios: in which intents you appear (selection/comparison/instructions/alternatives).
  • Response quality: correctness of facts, correctness of names, “correct” advantages.
  • Funnel: growth in brand search, transitions, conversions from AI traffic (if it is tracked).

When to expect the effect

Type of changes When we see the first signals When fixing stability
Articles / FAQ / criteria 1–3 weeks 4–8 weeks
Website/product pages 1–4 weeks 6–12 weeks
External sources (media/reviews) 2–6 weeks 8–16 weeks

How to build control: “prompt sets”

  1. Fixing a set of queries (20–50) and divide by intent.
  2. We measure the base: answers, sources, competitors, share of voice.
  3. Implementing changes (social/content/code) and mark the release date.
  4. Repeat the measurement using the same set, we compare “before/after”.
  5. We make alerts to critical changes (for example, the brand disappeared, the wording changed, the facts were replaced).

What we consider success

  • You showed up in target scenarios (and stay there over time).
  • AI correctly describes product and benefits, without errors.
  • Share of voice is growing in comparison with competitors.
  • Citation increases your reference URLs (guides/product pages/FAQ).
