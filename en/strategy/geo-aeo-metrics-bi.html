How to evaluate the effectiveness of a GEO/AEO strategy: key metrics and analysis system

GEO and AEO are a shift from “position in search” to “is there a brand in the neural network’s response and what does it sound like there.” Below is not a dry glossary, but a chain of living intentions: how the user thinks and how he collects the LLM response. At the end there is a summary table of metrics and a link to interactive BI dashboard with demo data.

No in answers What questions Weight in response What fragments Blind Spots How the role is changing Traffic quality Page Contribution Delay Knowledge node Summary

Today people often doesn't look for ten blue links — he asks a question to ChatGPT, Gemini or Perplexity and receives the finished text. The key success metric increasingly sounds like this: did we respond and in what role? — background, one of the options or a recommended solution.

Why doesn't my brand appear in responses from ChatGPT and other neural networks?

The first level is to understand whether the brand exists in a generative environment at all. We are not looking at the “mentioned” checkbox, but at the meaning:

  • whether the brand appears in LLM responses;
  • in what formulations it is described;
  • is it a recommendation, an example, or a random name;
  • Is it consistently repeated for similar requests?

How to measure: manual prompt tests using intent maps, regular semantic samples, comparison of responses from different models (ChatGPT / Gemini / Claude / Perplexity).

Main indicator: AI Visibility Rate — the proportion of requests for which the brand is the answer.

Important: models do not “search your site” line by line - they collect the answer from probabilistic fragments of knowledge (RAG, embeddings, retrieval).

On what issues do neural networks begin to recommend a brand?

The next level is not the fact of appearance, but the context in which the brand becomes relevant. Let's look at:

  • what issues “bind” the brand;
  • what topics have already been covered;
  • where the brand is systematic, and where it pops up randomly;
  • which choice scenarios are already occupied by competitors.

Typical formulations that create visibility: “what is better to choose...”, “how it works...”, “what alternatives...”, “which solution is suitable for...”, “comparison of options...”.

Metrics: Intent Coverage Score — the proportion of questions from the intent map where the brand is already present in the answers. This is not a “search” demand map, but semantic space LLM.

Why is the brand important in one answer, but almost inaudible in another?

We measure not “mentioned / not”, but weight inside the answer. The brand can be:

  • accidentally named;
  • in the list of options;
  • recommended solution;
  • a reference source of meaning.

What we are watching: position in lists, frequency in one answer, role (main / auxiliary / contextual).

Metrics: Answer Share Index — the conditional share of the “response space” occupied by the brand. This is close to how LLM redistributes the weight of entities rather than copying sources verbatim.

What pages and pieces of text actually end up in neural network responses?

LLMs rarely “take over the page.” More often removed chunks — paragraphs, tables, steps, FAQ. Therefore, it is not the URL for the sake of the URL that wins, but the specific block.

Let's analyze: which paragraphs are picked up more often, which pages become sources, which formats stick more consistently.

What usually works best are: FAQ, how-to, “X vs Y” comparisons, definitions, structured tables.

Metrics: Extraction Rate — how often content blocks are included in responses. This is RAG: retrieval logic at the fragment level, not "one document = one response".

Where does a brand have no visibility in AI, although there is clearly demand there?

Strategic layer - white spots. Let's look:

  • topics that are not closed at all;
  • requests where there are competitors, but you are not;
  • intentions that do not lead to the emergence of a brand.

Output: AI white spaces map, priorities for content production, growth points.

Metrics: Gap Index — the share of intentions where the brand is absent. This is the AI ​​version of keyword gap, but at the level meanings, and not just words.

How does the role of brand within responses change over time?

Not only is the point on the chart important today, but also trajectory: A brand can evolve from “one of a kind” to a recommendation to being the expert source in the category.

We track: tone of references, stability of position, context of use.

Metrics: Brand Authority Positioning Score — a generalized index of the brand’s “status” in the responses. Close in spirit to entity authority in knowledge graph.

Do neural networks bring traffic to the site that is at least something obligatory?

If the answer contains links or the user goes to the site himself, it is not raw visits that are important, but quality: depth, conversion, returns, post-transition behavior.

AI traffic often comes from people with already formed intent - closer to a solution than a random click from the search results.

Metrics: AI Traffic Quality Index — a composite index of the quality of visits from AI referrers / UTM / prompt campaigns. In industry reviews (Similarweb, Cloudflare, etc.), AI-referral segments often show higher conversion compared to classic SEO - but the picture depends on the niche and traffic marking.

Which pages contribute the most to AI visibility?

Not all content is created equal for LLM systems. We highlight:

  • pages with high citation rates in replies;
  • pages with multi-intent coverage;
  • pages that are more likely to be the source of fragments.

Result - list AI-core pages and understanding the content core of the site.

Metrics: AI Contribution Score — the contribution of the URL/cluster to the appearance of the brand and facts in the answers.

Why doesn't new material appear immediately on ChatGPT after publication?

There is no linear “published → tomorrow in reply” button in GEO. The delay depends on indexing, updating embeddings, and the frequency of rebuilding retrieval corpuses for a particular system.

We measure: time before appearing in answers, impact of updates, “lag” between the index and availability for generation.

Metrics: Indexation-to-AI Lag — delay from publication/update to stable appearance in AI responses. This is the key difference from classic SEO: index ≠ instant availability for generation.

When does a brand stop being a “site” and start being a knowledge hub for the model?

The final level - the brand consistently appears in different types of requests, is combined with other entities, and is reproduced in responses with different formulations of the problem.

Let's look: intersections of entities, joint mentions with other brands, sustainability of presence.

Metrics: Knowledge Graph Strength Score — how densely and diversely the brand is built into the “network of meanings” that the model can assemble. Similar to Google Knowledge Graph, Wikidata and entity-based retrieval.

Final GEO/AEO rating system

Efficiency is not just one number, but level set. Below is a summary table for BI and regular reports (the same series can be seen on dashboard).

Metrics What answers the question
AI Visibility Rate Visibility: how often does the brand appear in replies?
Intent Coverage Score Demand coverage based on the intent map
Answer Share Index Share of brand “place” within the answer
Extraction Rate Content extractability (chunks)
Gap Index Empty zones - where the brand is not in demand

The main conclusion - and what to do about it in practice

GEO/AEO is not “optimize your pages a little more.” This presence control in replies, semantic weight of the brand, extracted fragments and role within LLM knowledge systems.

Further down the funnel after the visibility audit: consolidate the metrics in the report, set up regular prompt runs and link them to the product roadmap of the content - see AI visibility audit, implementation of the website and content system and a live prototype GEO/AEO BI dashboard.
