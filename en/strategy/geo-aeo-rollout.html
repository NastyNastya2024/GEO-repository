Strategy for implementing a GEO/AEO website and content system

Below is the same implementation plan, but in the language of living intentions: as if this is not the table of contents of the manual, but a chain of questions asked by the marketer, product and neural network. Next - a knowledge map, pages, a “factory” of blocks, equipment and metrics; a detailed system of metrics and a demo dashboard are in separate materials.

Are we still ordering a “site for SEO”? Why in 2026 the main bet is not positions, but put forward answers

To be honest: the positions in the search results have not disappeared, but increasingly the user does not reach the point of clicking on ten links — he receives a ready-made answer in the chat or in the AI ​​search wrapper. Therefore, we do not design “pages for the sake of the crawler,” but retrievable response system for neural networks and next generation search.

Each element of the system must satisfy three simple tests:

  • it can be pulled out as an independent fragment of the answer;
  • it reads without “read the previous three paragraphs”;
  • its structure is unambiguous for both man and machine.

There is no content yet: where to really start - an AI audit, a map of questions and a graph of topics without self-deception?

While not a single line has been written, you can still burn your budget on beautiful text, if you haven’t recorded: what the model already “knows” about the brand, what questions you didn’t receive and how the topics are related to each other. Stage 1 is about an honest picture, and not about the technical specifications for copyright.

Does the brand appear in ChatGPT or does it not exist at all - what to remove during a zero audit?

Audit of current AI visibility — we fix:

  • what queries already bring the brand to LLM/search;
  • in what contexts the brand is mentioned;
  • which competitors dominate the responses;
  • what topics are not closed at all.

Stage result: map of current AI visibility.

Why do we need a map of questions instead of “two thousand lines of semantics”?

The classic core is useful for advertising and SEO, but is more important for response systems how people formulate problems. Therefore, instead of a list of “keys” we collect intent card — a set of real semantic queries:

  • Explain - What is this;
  • How-to - how to do it;
  • Compare — comparison of solutions;
  • Decision — what to choose;
  • Commercial — solutions and services.

Result: an intent map that replaces the SEO structure.

How not to leave topics as “islands” if the site should draw internal links and meaning?

We design knowledge graph in the human sense of the word - not necessarily in the triple-store database, but as a conscious network of pages. We define:

  • key nodes (main topics);
  • secondary nodes (subtopics);
  • connections between them.

Important: each topic must have at least 2-3 links; isolated content is excluded.

Result: website architecture as a knowledge graph.

What should the page look like if it is read not by “scrolling”, but by “cutting out paragraphs” in the model’s response?

The page is not a longread for the sake of volume, but container for fragments, which retrieval can raise separately. Therefore, above is the answer, below are evidence and formats that are easy to retell.

What should a person (and a model) see in the first seconds - without scrolling to the point?

A single principle for layout of meaning:

  • short direct answer at the beginning;
  • explanation blocks (atomic);
  • structured lists/steps/tables;
  • FAQ inside the page.

Why split the text into micro-blocks that live on their own?

Each block is a mini-contract with the reader and the model. He is obliged:

  • solve one specific micro-issue;
  • be autonomous (understandable separately);
  • be logically complete;
  • do not depend on neighboring sections.

What four types of URLs are almost always needed to close demand from explanation to purchase?

  • Answer Pages - direct answers;
  • How-to Pages — instructions;
  • Comparison Pages - comparisons;
  • Product/Service Pages - commercial entities.

Where can I get a stream of small answers without endless long reads “for the sake of coverage”?

“Content factory” sounds industrial, but in meaning it is editorial discipline: one intent - one released block, clear deadline and clear format. Otherwise, the team will drown in drafts, and the site will remain a beautiful showcase without extractable meanings.

Why is the unit of production a response block and not an “article as a file”?

Content is created not as articles, but as stream of atomic response blocks.

Each block: one intent, one meaning, one result.

What formats can the system produce consistently, and not “whatever happens to the author”?

  • explanatory blocks (definition-first);
  • instructions (step-based);
  • comparisons (table-driven);
  • FAQ answers (query-driven).

Where should the blocks be sent if “everything on the site” no longer works as the only channel?

  • Website — main knowledge base;
  • Social networks — distribution of explanations;
  • External platforms — increased AI visibility.

What exactly in the code and layout prevents neural networks and crawlers from “getting to the meaning”?

An ideal text is powerless if it hides behind hydration, tabs and empty div. The technical layer is part of GEO/AEO: delivering text so that it can be reliably read and indexed.

SSR, SSG or "thick client": which is closer to "text to HTML immediately"?

  • SSR/static generation;
  • minimizing client-side JS;
  • Availability of content without interactive dependencies.

Schema.org for show or a normal hierarchy of headings - what really feeds the model?

Types like Article, FAQPage, HowTo, Product - but the key thing is: not the markup itself, but the HTML structure, from which the meaning is read without the “magic” of scripts.

How to understand that the render is “clean”: DOM, hidden blocks and lazy text loading?

  • quick access to text;
  • no hidden blocks;
  • lack of critical lazy-load for the main text;
  • clean DOM structure.

What does the text sound like that the model wants to retell, and not “bypass”?

GEO is not “add a keyword.” This specific language: Fewer abstractions, more testable statements that are resistant to compression and paraphrasing.

What one-paragraph rules save both the editor and embeddings?

  • one meaning = one block;
  • without “marketing noise”;
  • without abstract formulations;
  • maximum specificity.

What does “retellability” mean: the model will compress the text, but the meaning will remain?

Content should:

  • easy to shorten without losing meaning;
  • maintain structure when paraphrasing;
  • be resistant to LLM extraction.

Why does the block need autonomy: it was pulled out of the page - is it still valuable?

Each block can be pulled out of the page - it will remain understandable and retain its value separately.

How can you make sure that pages don't argue with each other using the same words?

Cohesion is not “internal PageRank for the sake of SEO,” but single map of meaning: from definitions to deepening and back to the supporting topic. Otherwise, the user and model will receive different responses from different URLs.

Where should each page link to so the reader doesn't get stuck?

Each page:

  • refers to basic definitions;
  • goes into recesses;
  • returns to the main node.

How to agree on terms so that the site does not have five “different products” with the same name?

Semantic consistency: common terms, common definitions, absence of synonymous noise.

Launched - and what to watch in the first weeks: AI, content or human behavior?

Without metrics you are not managing the system, you are hope for luck. Minimum set: visibility in replies, block retrievability, AI traffic behavior. A detailed system of indicators and a demo dashboard are in the material "GEO/AEO and BI Metrics" and on the page interactive BI dashboard.

Do we appear in the answers and by what wording can we check this manually and on a schedule?

  • whether the brand appears in AI responses;
  • for what requests;
  • in what context.

Which pages and blocks actually “feed” responses – and which ones simply exist?

  • which blocks are retrieved more often;
  • which pages are cited;
  • which intents are closed.

Does AI traffic lead to depth and conversion - or are these random clicks?

  • involvement;
  • depth of interaction;
  • conversions from AI traffic.

At the end of the project: do we have a new website or a working knowledge factory for AI?

If everything is done wisely, what goes into production is not “a set of pages”, but knowledge infrastructure optimized for AI extraction — with an intent map, graph, block flow, technology and metrics.

It consists of:

  • intent map;
  • knowledge graph;
  • atomic content systems;
  • technically clean website;
  • AI visibility metrics (see also metrics and BI system).
